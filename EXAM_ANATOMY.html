Read the map like this

Bands above/below teeth = nerves for the soft tissue (palate, buccal gingiva, lingual). Color inside the tooth = nerve for the pulp + bone.

The two rules

Upper palate splits at the canine — front = NPN, back = GPN. Lower buccal splits at the mental foramen — front = Mental, back = LBN.

Yellow MB dot = the trap

Upper M1 (1.6 and 2.6) has a yellow "MB" dot. The mesio-buccal root escapes PSAN. Always add a plexal supplement there.

⚠️ The 5 traps that catch exam candidates

  1. Upper M1 MB root: PSAN misses it. MSAN takes it (when MSAN exists ~50%). Otherwise = plexal supplement at the MB root.
  2. Upper canine palate: NPN and GPN anastomose here. If NPN block isn't enough, add palatal infiltration distal to the canine.
  3. Upper centrals: ASAN crosses the midline. Add a trans-frenular plexal.
  4. Lower centrals: Incisive N. crosses the midline. Spix on one side isn't enough → add contralateral labial plexal.
  5. Lower molars: Mental N. STOPS at the mental foramen. Molar buccal = Long Buccal (LBN). Spix alone doesn't cover it.
