## 0. Trigeminal Nerve — Anatomy Refresher (LP 2)

- Largest cranial nerve (CN V). Mixed: sensory to face/mucosa + motor to muscles of mastication + proprioception.
- Exits pons (junction with middle cerebellar peduncle) → joins **Gasser (semilunar) ganglion** in **Meckel's cave** (lateral to cavernous sinus, in trigeminal depression of temporal bone).
- Three divisions:
  - **V1 Ophthalmic** — purely sensory. Branches: frontal, lacrimal, nasociliary. Innervates forehead/scalp, frontal & ethmoidal sinuses, upper eyelid + conjunctiva, cornea, dorsum of nose.
  - **V2 Maxillary** — purely sensory. Exits skull through **foramen rotundum** → pterygopalatine fossa → inferior orbital fissure → infraorbital groove/canal → renamed **infraorbital nerve** at face. 14 terminal branches; supplies lower eyelid, cheek, maxillary sinus, nasal cavity, upper lip, all maxillary teeth + gingiva, hard & soft palate.
  - **V3 Mandibular** — mixed. Both roots exit through **foramen ovale** into infratemporal fossa, then splits into:
    - **Anterior division** (mainly motor): muscles of mastication + buccal n (sensory).
    - **Posterior division** (mainly sensory): auriculotemporal, lingual, inferior alveolar (also motor branch to mylohyoid).

Key terminal branches you must block in the LP exam:
- V2: posterior superior alveolar (PSA), middle/anterior superior alveolar, infraorbital, greater palatine, nasopalatine.
- V3: inferior alveolar (+ terminal mental & incisive), lingual, buccal.

# A. PLEXAL / INFILTRATION ANESTHESIA

## 1. Plexus (Supraperiosteal) Anesthesia (LP 4)

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Indication | One or two adjacent teeth, mostly maxillary + frontal mandibular region. |
| Contraindications | Multiple adjacent teeth (use nerve block instead), acute inflammation/infection over the area, dense overlying bone. |
| Needle | **Short needle**. |
| Operator position | 9–10 o'clock. |
| Insertion site | Mucobuccal fold over the tooth, bevel toward bone. |
| Depth | Only a **few mm**. |
| Volume | ⅓ – ½ cartridge, deposited slowly over ~30 s. |
| Onset | 2–4 min, anesthetizes tooth + associated soft tissue. |

> Memory tip: plexal = "near the apex"; troncular = "near the trunk."

# B. MAXILLARY (V2) NERVE BLOCKS

## 2. PSA Block — Tuberosity Technique (LP 3)

**Anesthetized nerves:** Posterior superior alveolar nerve.

**Territory:** Pulp of M3, M2, M1 (except mesio-buccal root of M1) + periodontium, ligaments, alveolar bone, **buccal gingiva**, lateral & posterior wall of maxillary sinus.

**Landmarks**
- Bone: zygomatic process of the maxilla.
- Dental: mesio-buccal root of the upper M2.
- Soft tissue: mobile mucosa, superior fornix.

**Patient:** Head straight or slightly extended, mouth half-open.

**Puncture site:** Superior fornix mobile mucosa, **behind the zygomatic process**, near MB root of M2.

**Needle direction:** Upward 45° + inward + backward.

**Depth:** 10–15 mm, maintaining bone contact.

**Technique**
1. Disinfect mucosa (+ optional topical).
2. Tip oriented **toward bone** — do NOT sting periosteum.
3. **Aspirate mandatory** (pterygoid plexus risk).
4. Slow deposit while keeping bone contact.

**Accidents**
- Pain → fast deposit, cold solution, thick needle, broken tip, periosteum puncture.
- Needle breakage → manipulation failure.
- Vascular: **pterygoid venous plexus → tuberosity hematoma**.
- Prevention: thin needle, bone contact, slow injection, **always aspirate**.
- If positive aspiration: gauze in superior fornix + compression with thenar surface.

## 3. Infraorbital Nerve Block (LP 5)

**Anesthetized nerves:** Infraorbital n. + its anterior superior alveolar branch (and middle superior alveolar when present).

**Territory:** Lower eyelid, side of the nose, upper lip; maxillary incisors, canines & **premolars** (pulp, periosteum, vestibular bone & gingiva); part of maxillary sinus.

**Landmarks**
- Palpate the **infraorbital ridge** (patient looks straight ahead) — keep finger on the foramen for the whole procedure.

**Approach:** Intraoral (preferred).

**Puncture site:** Vestibular mucosa ~ 0.5 cm from the buccal surface, parallel to the long axis of the **first premolar**.

**Needle direction:** Parallel to long axis of first premolar, upward toward the foramen palpated by the finger.

**Depth:** ~1.5 – 2.5 cm (until near foramen).

**Volume:** 1.5 – 2 mL drawn; small test bolus to **palpate foramen painlessly**, then aspirate + slow inject.

---

## 4. Greater Palatine (Posterior Palatine) Block (LP 6)

**Anesthetized nerves:** Greater palatine nerve.

**Territory:** **Posterior 2/3 of the hard palate** + overlying soft tissues.

**Landmarks (posterior palatine foramen)**
- Bone: 0.5 cm anterior to the posterior edge of the hard palate; 1 cm anterior to pterygoid hamulus; in the dihedral angle between alveolar process and horizontal plate of palatine bone; 1 cm above & inside the lower edge of the alveolar ridge.
- Dental: 1 cm medial to the upper M3 (or temporary M2 in children).
- Soft tissue: 1 cm from the gingival festoon of M3; mucoperiosteum looks paler/funnel-shaped over the foramen.

**Patient:** Head extended, mouth wide open.

**Puncture site:** Palatine mucoperiosteum, 1 cm from the alveolar ridge, past the upper M2.

**Needle direction:** Upward + backward + outward (syringe coming from opposite commissure). **Do not enter the duct.**

**Volume:** Only 0.2 – 0.3 mL.

**Pros:** No need to penetrate foramen; tiny volume; <1% positive aspiration.
**Cons:** Possible bleeding; potentially traumatic.

---

## 5. Nasopalatine Block (Incisive / Scarpa) (LP 7)

**Anesthetized nerves:** Nasopalatine (incisive) nerve.

**Territory:** **Anterior 1/3 of the hard palate** (soft + hard tissues, both sides if you enter the V/Y duct).

**Landmarks (anterior palatine / incisive foramen)**
- Bone: on the median palatine line, 1 cm behind the anterior alveolar ridge.
- Soft tissue: 1 cm from the gingival festoon, **covered by the retroincisal papilla**.
- Dental: median line between the two upper central incisors.

**Patient:** Head extended, mouth wide open.

**Puncture site:** Palatal mucosa **lateral to the incisive papilla** — never sting the papilla itself (very vascular, very painful).

**Technique:** Puncture, then advance the needle into the duct **5–6 mm**.

**Side of approach:** Always from the **side opposite** to what you want to block (because of the V/Y shape of the duct — by entering from one side you cross to the other).

**Needle direction:** Upward + backward + outward toward the side to block.

**Volume:** 0.2 – 0.3 mL.

**Pros:** Minimal volume; minimal penetration; <1% positive aspiration.
**Cons:** Painful if papilla pierced; bleeding risk.

**Extraoral variants**
- *Infiltration:* needle into nostril at nasal floor–septum angle, 1.5 cm behind nasal orifice, on the side to block; deposit just under the mucosa of nasal floor; direction downward + backward.
- *Topical (Escat):* compress soaked in anesthetic placed 1.5 cm inside the nose for several minutes — exploits superficial position of NP nerve on the nasal floor.

# C. MANDIBULAR (V3) NERVE BLOCKS

## 6. Inferior Alveolar + Lingual Block — Spix's Spine Technique (LP 8)

> Most common mandibular block. **Highest failure rate: 15–20%.** Success needs deposit ≤ 1 mm from the nerve trunk.

**Anesthetized nerves**
- Inferior alveolar nerve (IAN) + terminal branches: **mental n.**, **incisive n.**.
- Lingual nerve.

**Territory**
- All teeth on one mandibular hemiarch + periodontium + bone.
- Half of the lip.
- Buccal mucoperiosteum from the mental foramen to the midline (mental n.).
- Lingual gingiva + periosteum to the midline (lingual n.).
- Anterior 2/3 of the tongue + floor of mouth (lingual n.).

**Landmarks of the mandibular foramen**
- **Bone:** center of the medial side of the mandibular ramus, ½ distance between anterior–posterior margins and between sigmoid notch and lower edge, **posterior to the lingula (Spix's spine)**.
- **Soft tissue (intraoral):** lateral to the pterygomandibular fold/raphe, medial to the temporal crest; **1 cm above the occlusal plane**.
- **Soft tissue (extraoral):** halfway between tragus and the anterior insertion of masseter on mandible.

**Puncture site (intraoral):** Lateral to pterygomandibular fold, medial to temporal crest, **1 cm above occlusal plane**.

**Needle direction:** **Posterior + lateral + horizontal**. Syringe body lying over the **opposite premolars**.

**Depth:** Advance **2 – 2.5 cm** maintaining bone contact.

**Direct technique (the one you'll perform)**
1. Disinfect mucosa (± topical).
2. Puncture at the injection site (see above).
3. Syringe body at the **opposite-side premolars**.
4. Advance horizontal + outward + posterior, 2–2.5 cm to bone contact.
5. **Aspirate** → if negative, slow deposit ⇒ **T1: anesthetize the IAN**.
6. Withdraw needle **1 cm**.
7. Aspirate again → negative → slow deposit ⇒ **T2: anesthetize the lingual n.**

**Indirect technique**
1. Syringe parallel to the **same** quadrant.
2. Advance to bone contact at the temporal crest.
3. Move syringe to the **opposite** side.
4. Advance to bone contact above the mandibular foramen.

**Accidents**
- Pain (fast deposit, cold, thick needles, bevel up, alcohol traces, subperiosteal deposit).
- Needle breakage (sudden direction change, full needle insertion).
- Vascular / hematoma / neuritis.
- **Facial palsy (hemiparesis)**: needle passed posterior to ramus → anesthetic into the **parotid** → CN VII anesthesia. Cause: failure to find bone contact (passed the mandible posteriorly).
- Tip: bevel oriented to bone to avoid periosteal trauma.

## 7. Mental Foramen Nerve Block (LP 9)

**Anesthetized nerves:** Mental n. + incisive n.

**Territory**
- Mental n.: half of the lower lip, skin/soft tissues of chin, vestibular gingival mucosa from the mental foramen to the midline.
- Incisive n.: teeth from the mental foramen to the midline + periodontium + bone (i.e., premolar → central incisor).

**Landmarks of the mental foramen**
- **Bone:** anterior external side of the mandibular horizontal ramus, above the start of the external oblique line; half-distance between basilar and alveolar ridge (dentate); on the vertical line connecting supraorbital ↔ infraorbital ↔ mental foramina. In edentate atrophy, foramen migrates closer to (or onto) the ridge.
- **Dental:** between the roots of the two lower premolars (~50%); past lower P2 (25%); past root of lower P1 (20%); distal to P2 (25%). Rare double foramen.
- **Soft tissue:** vertical through the buccal commissure; vertical through the medial canthus of the eye; 0.5 cm medial to the mid-pupillary line when patient looks ahead.

**Intraoral technique**
- Puncture: mobile mucosa, **mucobuccal fold past the mesial root of the lower M1**.
- Direction: **downward + forward + inward**.

**Extraoral technique**
- Puncture: in the cheek, on a horizontal line through the oral commissure, **1.5 – 2 cm behind the commissure**.
- Direction: **downward + inward + forward**.

---

## 8. Lingual Nerve — Isolated Block (LP 10)

**Anesthetized nerve:** Lingual n. (branch of V3 posterior division; originates beneath lateral pterygoid, ~1 cm anterior + medial to IAN; becomes superficial **distal to lower M3** under floor-of-mouth mucosa — surgical danger zone).

**Territory:** Anterior 2/3 of tongue, oral floor, lingual gingiva from midline to M3.

> Note: the lingual n. is most often anesthetized as a **byproduct of the Spix block**. The two techniques below are for isolated cases.

### 8a. Lingual block at the level of M3
- Landmarks: **lower M3, paralingual fold/tongue margin, angle of mandible**.
- Insertion: next to lower M3, paralingual fold, ½ distance between gingiva and base of tongue, anterior + medial to mandibular angle.
- Direction: **downward + outward + posterior**.
- Depth: **0.5 cm**.

### 8b. Dan Theodorescu technique (at the canine/P2)
- Landmarks: canine or P2, paralingual fold, lingual surface of the mandible.
- Insertion: angle between oral floor and lingual surface of mandible, next to canine/P2.
- Depth: **0.5 cm**.

---

## 9. Buccal Nerve Block (LP 11)

**Anesthetized nerve:** Buccal n. (branch of the **anterior division** of V3; descends medial + anterior to lingual & IAN; curves laterally at base of coronoid process).

**Territory:** Vestibular gingiva from lower M3 → mental foramen (M3, M2, M1, P1); mucosa of the cheek.

### 9a. Classical Buccal Block
- **Landmarks:** Stensen duct papilla, anterior margin of mandibular ramus, **maxillary occlusal plane**.
- **Insertion site:** intersection of the maxillary occlusal plane with the anterior margin of the mandibular ramus; distal & vestibular to **upper M3**; 1 cm posterior and inferior to the Stensen duct.
- **Direction:** **posterior + parallel to upper occlusal plane + laterally (outward)**.

### 9b. Vestibular Infiltration (simpler alternative)
- Insertion: vestibular mucosa, distal & vestibular to **lower M3**.
- Direction: **posterior + horizontal + parallel to the lower occlusal plane**.

# E. GENERAL TECHNIQUE COMMANDMENTS (apply to every block)

1. **Disinfect** mucosa (alcohol or CHX).
2. **± topical anesthesia.**
3. **Bevel oriented toward bone** (prevents periosteum tearing & sub-periosteal deposit).
4. **Maintain bone contact** when block is "against bone" (PSA, Spix); never sting periosteum.
5. **Aspirate mandatory** before every deposit — repeat after any needle movement.
6. **Slow deposit** (≥ 30 s per cartridge fraction) to avoid pain & systemic load.
7. **Never insert needle to the hub** — leave ≥ 5 mm exposed (breakage retrieval).
8. **No sudden direction changes** mid-shaft (breakage).
9. **Patient position:** head straight/extended, mouth half-open (PSA) or wide open (palatal, Spix).
10. Document onset time, volume, side; check anesthesia depth before proceeding.

# F. WHAT TO REVISE THE NIGHT BEFORE

- The **Spix technique** front-to-back — landmarks, direction, depth, **T1/T2 sequence**, the parotid/facial-palsy accident.
- Distinguish **greater palatine vs. nasopalatine** (posterior 2/3 vs anterior 1/3; entry-from-opposite-side rule for NP).
- **Tuberosity hematoma** prevention & management (always aspirate).
- Which teeth the **PSA block misses** → **MB root of M1** (needs supplementation, often with plexus / infraorbital MSA).
- The two **lingual-only** techniques (M3 paralingual vs Dan Theodorescu) — both depth 0.5 cm.
- Buccal block: insertion at the **upper occlusal plane × anterior border of ramus**, not on the lower arch.
- Trigeminal foramina trio: **rotundum (V2), ovale (V3), Meckel's cave (ganglion)**.
