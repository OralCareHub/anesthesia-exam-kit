# Extraction Anesthesia Map — All 32 Teeth (FDI)

Source: Notes 21 Apr 2026 + 14 May 2026 + Anesthesia Techniques table + Puncture Sites diagram + STUDY_SUMMARY_LAB.md.

> **Rule of three for every extraction.** You always need to anesthetize:
> 1. **Pulp + PDL + alveolar bone** of the tooth (deep innervation).
> 2. **Vestibular (buccal/labial) gingiva + periosteum**.
> 3. **Oral (palatal in maxilla / lingual in mandible) gingiva + periosteum**.
> A buccal-only block extracts no tooth — you cannot elevate the palatal/lingual flap.

---

## 0. Quick legend — the 8 target nerves

| Abbrev | Full name | Division | Territory headline |
|---|---|---|---|
| **PSAN** | Posterior superior alveolar n. | V2 | M1 (NOT MB root), M2, M3 — pulp + buccal gingiva |
| **MSAN** | Middle superior alveolar n. | V2 *(inconstant)* | MB root of M1 + P1 + P2 |
| **ASAN** | Anterior superior alveolar n. | V2 | Canine + incisors (pulp + labial) |
| **GPN** | Greater (posterior) palatine n. | V2 | Posterior 2/3 hard palate (P1 → M3 palatal) |
| **NPN** | Nasopalatine (Scarpa) n. | V2 | Anterior 1/3 hard palate (canine → canine palatal) |
| **IAN** | Inferior alveolar n. (+ mental + incisive) | V3 | Whole mand. hemiarch pulp/bone; buccal gingiva P→I via mental |
| **LN** | Lingual n. | V3 | All mand. lingual gingiva + ant. 2/3 tongue + floor |
| **LBN** | Long buccal n. | V3 (ant. div.) | Mand. buccal gingiva from M1 → M3 (cheek mucosa) |

**Anastomosis rule (the trap):**
- **MB root of M1 ↔ MSAN** (when MSAN is absent → PSA block alone misses the MB root → plexal supplement at 1.6/2.6 MB).
- **NPN ↔ GPN at the level of P1 (canine in some sources)** — for upper canine/P1 palatal work, both palatal nerves may need blocking.
- **Mandibular incisors cross the midline** — IAN block on one side often needs a supplemental labial plexal at the opposite central incisor.

## 1. MAXILLA — Quadrant 1 (Upper Right) & Quadrant 2 (Upper Left)

Q1 and Q2 are mirror images — same nerve targets, just opposite side.

### 1.1 / 2.1 — Central incisor

| Component | Nerve | Block |
|---|---|---|
| Pulp + labial bone + labial gingiva | **ASAN** | **Plexal (supraperiosteal)** in vestibular fornix above apex — or **IO block** at the IO foramen (no need to enter canal) |
| Palatal gingiva + periosteum | **NPN** | **Local infiltration** lateral to incisive papilla OR **NPN block** at the anterior palatine (incisive) foramen, needle advanced 5–6 mm |
| Midline crossover | contralateral ASAN | **Trans-frenular plexal** on upper labial frenulum (covers 1.1 ↔ 2.1 crossover fibers) |

**Recommended extraction protocol:** Plexal 1.1 labial + NPN block from opposite-side entry + optional trans-frenular if midline numbness incomplete.

### 1.2 / 2.2 — Lateral incisor

| Component | Nerve | Block |
|---|---|---|
| Pulp + labial | **ASAN** | Plexal at 1.2 apex (vestibular fornix, supraperiosteal) — or IO block |
| Palatal | **NPN** | Local palatal infiltration OR NPN block |

### 1.3 / 2.3 — Canine

| Component | Nerve | Block |
|---|---|---|
| Pulp + buccal | **ASAN** | Plexal at 1.3 (canine fossa, above and slightly distal to apex) — or IO block |
| Palatal | **NPN ± GPN** (anastomosis sits here) | NPN block; if anesthesia incomplete add palatal infiltration (GPN territory begins) |

### 1.4 / 2.4 — First premolar

| Component | Nerve | Block |
|---|---|---|
| Pulp + buccal | **MSAN** (if present) — else **ASAN/PSAN overlap** | Plexal at 1.4 buccal — or **IO block** (IO covers ASA, and MSA if present — reliable for P1) |
| Palatal | **GPN** (note NPN anastomosis at P1 — both palatal n. may need infiltration) | Local palatal infiltration 1 cm from gingival margin OR GPN block at greater palatine foramen |

### 1.5 / 2.5 — Second premolar

| Component | Nerve | Block |
|---|---|---|
| Pulp + buccal | **MSAN** (if present) — else PSAN overlap | Plexal at 1.5 buccal (puncture site for IO landmark sits here — 2–2.5 cm above the apex) — or IO block |
| Palatal | **GPN** | Local palatal infiltration OR GPN block |

### 1.6 / 2.6 — First molar ⚠️ TRAP TOOTH

PSA does NOT supply the MB root → PSA block alone leaves the MB root sensitive.

| Component | Nerve | Block |
|---|---|---|
| Pulp DB + Palatal roots | **PSAN** | **PSA tuberosity block** (depth 10–15 mm, up + in + back, MANDATORY aspiration — pterygoid plexus) |
| Pulp **MB root** | **MSAN** (when absent → only supplied by plexal twigs) | **Plexal supplement at MB root of 1.6** (vestibular fornix above MB root, dental landmark = MB root upper M1) — this is the classic "MSA top-up" |
| Buccal gingiva | PSAN (+ MSAN if present) | Covered by PSA + plexal supplement above |
| Palatal | **GPN** | GPN block at greater palatine foramen (1 cm medial to M3, palatal mucosa, syringe from opposite commissure) |

**Extraction protocol for 1.6:** PSA tuberosity + plexal MB root + GPN. Three injections, non-negotiable.

### 1.7 / 2.7 — Second molar

| Component | Nerve | Block |
|---|---|---|
| Pulp + buccal | **PSAN** | PSA tuberosity block (landmark: behind zygomatic process, near MB root of upper M2 — M2 is THE landmark tooth) |
| Palatal | **GPN** | GPN block |

### 1.8 / 2.8 — Third molar

| Component | Nerve | Block |
|---|---|---|
| Pulp + buccal | **PSAN** | PSA tuberosity block |
| Palatal | **GPN** | GPN block (foramen sits 1 cm medial to M3 — directly above your target) |

## 2. MANDIBLE — Quadrant 3 (Lower Left) & Quadrant 4 (Lower Right)

Lower-jaw bone is dense → plexal anesthesia fails on molars and is unreliable on premolars. **Default to truncular blocks.**

### 3.1 / 4.1 — Central incisor

| Component | Nerve | Block |
|---|---|---|
| Pulp + labial bone | **IAN → incisive branch** | IAN (Spix) block on the same side — but cross-midline fibers from the opposite incisive n. often leave residual sensation |
| Labial gingiva | **IAN → mental branch** | Covered by Spix or mental block |
| Lingual gingiva + bone | **LN** | Covered by Spix (T2 deposit after withdrawing 1 cm) |
| Midline supplementation | contralateral incisive | **Plexal labial infiltration** at the contralateral central incisor — or bilateral mental block for safety |

**Lighter alternative for non-surgical anteriors:** bilateral plexal labial + lingual infiltration may suffice for simple intact-crown extractions; Spix is the safer choice when bone is thicker or root is curved.

### 3.2 / 4.2 — Lateral incisor

Same as 3.1/4.1: **IAN (Spix) covering pulp/labial via incisive + mental, + LN (via Spix T2), + contralateral plexal labial if midline numbness incomplete.**

### 3.3 / 4.3 — Canine

| Component | Nerve | Block |
|---|---|---|
| Pulp + buccal | **IAN (incisive branch) + mental branch** | **Spix block** OR **mental + incisive block** at the mental foramen (foramen sits between roots of P1–P2 — covers P→I) |
| Lingual | **LN** | Covered by Spix T2; if mental block is used alone, supplement with lingual infiltration or Theodorescu lingual block at canine/P2 |

### 3.4 / 4.4 — First premolar

| Component | Nerve | Block |
|---|---|---|
| Pulp | **IAN** | Spix block |
| Buccal gingiva | **IAN → mental branch** (mental foramen sits at this level) | Covered by Spix; alternatively mental + incisive block |
| Lingual gingiva | **LN** | Spix T2 (or Theodorescu technique at canine/P2 for isolated LN) |

### 3.5 / 4.5 — Second premolar

| Component | Nerve | Block |
|---|---|---|
| Pulp | **IAN** | Spix block (landmark for the Dan Theodorescu lingual technique — 2nd premolar) |
| Buccal gingiva | **IAN → mental branch** | Covered by Spix; mental + incisive block as alternative |
| Lingual gingiva | **LN** | Spix T2 |

### 3.6 / 4.6 — First molar

| Component | Nerve | Block |
|---|---|---|
| Pulp + lingual aspect of buccal bone | **IAN** | Spix block (depth 2–2.5 cm, 1 cm above occlusal plane, syringe over opposite premolars) |
| Buccal gingiva + cheek | **LBN** ⚠️ (NOT mental — mental ends at the mental foramen, anterior to M1) | **Long buccal block** at base of coronoid process OR vestibular infiltration distal to lower M3 |
| Lingual | **LN** | Spix T2 |

**Extraction protocol for any mand. molar:** Spix + Long Buccal. Three nerves blocked (IAN + LN + LBN) with two injections.

### 3.7 / 4.7 — Second molar

Same protocol as 3.6/4.6: **Spix (IAN + LN) + Long Buccal**.

### 3.8 / 4.8 — Third molar

Same protocol: **Spix (IAN + LN) + Long Buccal**.

For the LN at the M3 region: it becomes superficial just distal to lower M3 in the floor-of-mouth mucosa — surgical danger zone, easy isolated infiltration if needed (0.5 cm depth, paralingual fold).

## 4. WHY-IT-MATTERS NOTES (the traps your examiner will probe)

1. **Why MB root of 1.6/2.6 needs a separate plexal?** PSAN bypasses it; MSAN (when present) is the sole supplier. If MSAN is absent, only plexal twigs reach it.
2. **Why mandibular molars need a long buccal block?** The mental nerve's buccal gingival territory ends at the mental foramen (≈ between premolars). Molar buccal gingiva is **long buccal n.** territory — Spix does NOT cover it.
3. **Why NPN block is approached from the opposite side?** The nasopalatine canal is V/Y-shaped — entering from one side crosses to the contralateral nerve trunk.
4. **Why GPN block uses only 0.2–0.3 mL?** Tiny submucosal pocket on hard palate, mucosa is paler/funnel-shaped at the foramen — over-injection causes ischemic palatal necrosis.
5. **Why aspirate twice in Spix block?** T1 (IAN deposit at bone contact, 2–2.5 cm) and T2 (LN deposit after 1 cm withdrawal) are anatomically distinct deposit sites — each requires its own aspiration.
6. **Why mandibular incisor extractions often fail with unilateral Spix?** Midline crossover from contralateral incisive nerve fibers — supplement with plexal labial at the opposite central.
7. **PSA-block hematoma:** pterygoid venous plexus is the danger. Mandatory aspiration; if positive → gauze + thenar compression in superior fornix.
8. **Spix-block facial palsy:** needle passed posterior to the ramus → anesthetic into parotid → CN VII anesthesia. Cause = failure to find bone contact. Always confirm bone before depositing.

## 5. INJECTION PARAMETERS QUICK-RECALL (from your handwritten table)

| Block | Depth | Volume | Site |
|---|---|---|---|
| Plexal (any tooth) | submucosal + supraperiosteal, few mm | 0.6 mL | Vestibular fornix above apex |
| PSA tuberosity | 10–15 mm, bone contact | — | Buccal sulcus near MB root of upper M2 |
| IO block (intraoral) | 1.5–2.5 cm to foramen | 1.5–2 mL drawn | Vestibular ~ 0.5 cm from MB root of first molar |
| IO block (extraoral) | to foramen | 0.2 mL after aspiration + locate + 0.2 mL more | 1.5–2 cm posterior to commissure of mouth, on line through nasolabial groove |
| GPN block | submucosal | 0.2–0.3 mL — do NOT enter canal | 1 cm medial to upper M3 alveolar ridge |
| NPN block | 5 mm into canal | 0.2–0.3 mL | Lateral to incisive papilla, opposite-side approach |
| IAN (Spix) | 2–2.5 cm bone contact | 1–1.7 mL | Lateral to pterygomandibular fold, 1 cm above occlusal plane |
| Lingual (Spix T2) | withdraw 1 cm from IAN site | — | Same approach as Spix |
| Lingual isolated (M3) | 0.5 cm | — | Paralingual fold next to lower M3 |
| Lingual (Dan Theodorescu) | 0.5 cm | — | Floor/mandible angle at canine or P2 |
| Mental + incisive (intraoral) | locate foramen, fix needle | 0.2 mL test + 0.2 mL deposit | Vestibular fornix, mesio-buccal root of lower M1 |
| Mental + incisive (extraoral) | locate foramen | 0.2 mL test + 0.2 mL deposit | 1.5–2 cm posterior to commissure |
| Long buccal | 2–4 mm | 0.3–0.5 mL | Anterior edge of vertical ramus, base of coronoid process |
| Buccal infiltration | submucosal + supraperiosteal | 0.3–0.5 mL | Distal & vestibular to lower M3 |
