⚠️ Anastomosis traps
Trap 1MSAN ↔ PSAN at MB root of M1

PSA block bypasses the MB root. When MSAN is absent (~50%), only plexal twigs reach it. Always add a plexal supplement at the MB root of upper M1.

Trap 2NPN ↔ GPN at the upper canine / P1

NPN territory ends and GPN begins around the upper canine. For canine palatal work, sometimes BOTH palatal nerves need supplementation — palatal infiltration handles it.

Trap 3ASAN ↔ contralateral ASAN at upper centrals

ASA fibers cross the midline at the centrals. Plexal alone leaves residual sensation → add a trans-frenular plexal.

Trap 4Incisive ↔ contralateral Incisive at lower centrals

Lower incisive fibers cross the midline. Spix on one side alone often leaves residual sensation at the opposite central → supplement with a contralateral labial plexal.

Trap 5Mental stops at the foramen — LBN takes over

Mental n. covers buccal gingiva only from foramen → midline (I, C, P1). Molar buccal gingiva is Long Buccal territory. Spix alone is NOT enough for a lower molar extraction.
