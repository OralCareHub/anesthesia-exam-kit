2 · Trigeminal Nerve I — V1 & V2

Nerves
V1, V2 + branches
Territory
Forehead → midface

CN V exits the pons → Gasser ganglion in Meckel's cave → V1 through sup. orbital fissure, V2 through foramen rotundum.

PA-3 · ANATOMY

3 · Trigeminal Nerve II — V3 Mandibular

Nerves
V3 (anterior + posterior trunk)
Territory
Mandible, tongue, masticatory mm.

V3 exits through foramen ovale. Anterior trunk = mostly motor + buccal (sensitive). Posterior trunk = auriculotemporal, lingual, inferior alveolar.

PA-4 · GEN

4 · Local & Loco-regional Infiltration

Nerves
Local terminations
Territory
1–2 teeth + soft tissue
Direction
bevel → bone
Depth
few mm
Volume
⅓–½ cartridge

Plexal/supraperiosteal: most common, works on all maxillary teeth (except upper M1) and lower anterior to mental foramen. Onset 2–4 min.

PA-5 · V2 maxillary

5 · PSA — Tuberosity Technique

Nerves
Posterior Superior Alveolar (PSA)
Territory
M3, M2, M1 (≠ MB root) + buccal gingiva + post./lat. wall of max. sinus
Direction
↑ 45° + in + back
Depth
10–15 mm

Insertion in superior fornix, behind zygomatic process, near MB root of M2. Mandatory aspiration — pterygoid venous plexus can give a tuberosity hematoma.

6 · MSA — Middle Superior Alveolar

Nerves
Middle Superior Alveolar (inconstant)
Territory
MB root M1, P1, P2 + their periodontium + lateral wall max. sinus
Direction
bevel → bone
Depth
few mm
Volume
½ cart.

When present, the MSA fills the PSA's gap (MB root of M1). Best blocked by plexal anesthesia in the mucobuccal fold over the premolars.

PA-7 · V2 maxillary

7 · ASA / Infraorbital Nerve Block

Nerves
Anterior Superior Alveolar + Infraorbital
Territory
Upper canine, lateral & central incisors + periodontium + vestibular gingiva + lower eyelid, side of nose, upper lip
Direction
parallel to long axis P1
Depth
1.5–2.5 cm
Volume
1.5–2 mL

Palpate the infraorbital ridge, keep your finger on the foramen for the whole procedure. Insert ~0.5 cm from buccal surface of P1, advance toward foramen.

PA-8 · V2 maxillary

8 · Palatine Fibromucosa — Greater Palatine + Nasopalatine

Nerves
Greater palatine + Nasopalatine (Scarpa)
Territory
GP → posterior 2/3 hard palate · NP → anterior 1/3 hard palate
Direction
GP: up + back + out (no duct) · NP: up + back + out from opposite side, enter duct 5–6 mm
Volume
0.2–0.3 mL each

GP foramen: 1 cm medial to upper M3, in the dihedral angle of palatine bone. NP: lateral to incisive papilla — never sting the papilla.

PA-9 · V3 mandibular

9 · Inferior Alveolar — Spix's Spine

Nerves
IAN (+ mental + incisive) + lingual via T2
Territory
All teeth one hemiarch + ½ lip + ant 2/3 tongue + floor
Direction
posterior + lateral + horizontal
Depth
2–2.5 cm → −1 cm for lingual

Most common mandibular block but ~15–20% failure. Lateral to ptg-mand fold, 1 cm above occlusal plane. Syringe body at opposite premolars. T1 = IAN at bone, T2 = lingual after withdrawing 1 cm.

PA-10 · V3 mandibular

10 · Mental & Incisive Nerve Block

Nerves
Mental + incisive
Territory
½ lower lip + chin · vestibular gingiva foramen → midline · teeth P → I
Direction
↓ + forward + inward

Mental foramen between roots of lower P1 + P2 in ~50%. Insertion at mucobuccal fold past mesial root of lower M1.

PA-11 · V3 mandibular

11 · Lingual Nerve Block

Nerves
Lingual nerve
Territory
Anterior 2/3 tongue + oral floor + lingual gingiva (midline → M3)
Direction
↓ + outward + posterior
Depth
0.5 cm

Usually anesthetized as Spix-T2. Isolated techniques: paralingual fold at M3, or Dan Theodorescu at canine/P2. Both depth 0.5 cm.

PA-12 · V3 mandibular

12 · Buccal Nerve Block

Nerves
Buccal (anterior trunk of V3)
Territory
Vestibular gingiva lower M3 → mental foramen + cheek mucosa
Direction
post + parallel to upper occlusal + lateral

Insertion is on the UPPER arch (occlusal plane × ant. ramus, distal to upper M3), even though the territory is lower-arch buccal gingiva.

13 · Simultaneous Techniques (Weisbrem, Gow-Gates, Vazirani-Akinosi)

Nerves
IAN + lingual + buccal in one puncture
Territory
Full hemimandible
Direction
varies by technique
Depth
varies

High-value alternatives when Spix fails: Weisbrem (Berg) — at mandibular tuberosity. Gow-Gates — at the condyle neck. Vazirani-Akinosi — closed-mouth approach.
