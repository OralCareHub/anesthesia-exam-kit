📋 Exam Cheat Sheet

Every technique, exam-only. Landmarks · steps · depth · volume · trap.

Color key — grouped by anatomical family
Green — Infiltration / plexal (universal, few mm)
Orange — V2 buccal blocks (PSA, Infraorbital)
Amber — V2 palate blocks (GPN, NPN)
Blue — V3 deep / truncular (Spix, Mental)
Indigo — V3 superficial (Long Buccal, Lingual)

🎯 The 10 commandments (apply to EVERY block)

  • Disinfect mucosa
  • Bevel toward bone
  • Maintain bone contact when block is "against bone" (PSA, Spix)
  • Aspirate before every deposit
  • Slow deposit ≥30 s per cartridge fraction
  • Never to the hub — leave ≥5 mm exposed
  • No mid-shaft direction changes (breakage)
  • Head position: straight/extended, mouth half-open (PSA) or wide open (palatal, Spix)
  • Aspirate again after any needle re-position
  • Wait for onset before proceeding

THE mandibular workhorse — failure rate 15–20%
V3 deep
IAN + mental + incisive + Lingual · all 16 lower
📍
WhereLateral to pterygomandibular fold, 1 cm above occlusal plane. Syringe over opposite premolars.

🌙 Morning-of-exam re-read
4 questions you WILL get
If you only remember 4 things — remember these
  1. Spix: syringe over opposite premolars → 2–2.5 cm to bone → T1 IAN → withdraw 1 cm → T2 Lingual. No bone contact = parotid = facial palsy.
  2. GPN vs NPN: posterior 2/3 palate = GPN (1 cm medial to M3). Anterior 1/3 = NPN (lateral to papilla, enter from opposite side).
  3. PSA: ALWAYS aspirate (pterygoid plexus → hematoma). Misses MB root of upper M1 — add plexal MB.
  4. Lingual isolated: both variants (M3 paralingual + Theodorescu at P2) = 0.5 cm depth. Same volume.
BonusTrigeminal foramina: rotundum (V2), ovale (V3), Meckel's cave (Gasser ganglion).
