🔗 Nerve → Block

10 nerves paired with the exact technique that blocks each. In order of exam use.

🎯 Read left column (nerve), commit right column (block)
A · V2 Superior alveolar (pulp + buccal)
1
PSAN
Posterior Superior Alveolar Nerve
V2
Innervates: pulp + buccal of upper M1(≠MB), M2, M3.
🎯 The block
PSA — Tuberosity block
📍
WhereSuperior fornix, behind zyg process, at MB root of upper M2
🧭
DirectionUp 45° + in + back
📏
Depth10–15 mm to bone
💧
Volume~1 mL
RememberPSA MISSES the MB root of M1 — always add a plexal supplement there.
🚨 MUST aspirate — pterygoid plexus → hematoma.
2
MSAN
Middle Superior Alveolar Nerve (~50% present)
V2
Innervates: pulp + buccal of upper P1, P2 + MB root of M1.
🎯 The block
Infraorbital block (covers ASA + MSA when present)
📍
WhereVestibular at upper P1, finger on IO foramen
📏
Depth1.5–2.5 cm to foramen
💧
Volume1.5–2 mL
AltWhen MSAN is absent → plexal supplement at MB root of M1 (0.6 mL).
3
ASAN
Anterior Superior Alveolar Nerve
V2
Innervates: pulp + labial of upper I + C.
🎯 The block
Plexal (supraperiosteal) at apex — or IO block
📍
WhereMucobuccal fold above the target tooth apex
📏
DepthFew mm (submucosal)
💧
Volume0.6 mL
TrapCrosses the midline at centrals → trans-frenular plexal top-up.
B · V2 Palate
4
GPN
Greater Palatine Nerve
V2
Innervates: posterior 2/3 hard palate — palate of upper P + M.
🎯 The block
Greater Palatine block
📍
WherePalate 1 cm medial to upper M3 (paler funnel-shape)
📏
DepthSubmucosal — NO canal
💧
Volume0.2–0.3 mL ONLY
🚨 Over-injection = ischemic palatal necrosis. Stop at blanching.
5
NPN
Nasopalatine Nerve (Scarpa's Nerve)
V2
Innervates: anterior 1/3 hard palate — palate of upper I + C.
🎯 The block
Nasopalatine block (incisive foramen)
📍
WhereLateral to incisive papilla — never through it
📏
Depth5–6 mm into duct
💧
Volume0.2–0.3 mL
RememberV/Y duct — enter from the OPPOSITE side of the target tooth.
C · V3 Pulp + bone (IAN family)
6
IAN
Inferior Alveolar Nerve
V3
Innervates: pulp + bone of all 16 lower teeth on one hemiarch.
🎯 The block
Spix's spine — IAN block (T1)
📍
WhereLateral to pterygomandibular fold, 1 cm above occlusal plane. Syringe over opposite premolars.
📏
Depth2–2.5 cm to bone
💧
Volume1–1.7 mL (T1 + T2)
RememberT1 at bone = IAN → withdraw 1 cm → T2 = Lingual. Two aspirations.
🚨 No bone contact = needle in parotid = facial palsy.
7
Incisive n.
Terminal branch of IAN (continues inside bone)
V3
Innervates: pulp + bone of lower I + C + P1 (from mental foramen to midline).
🎯 The block
Mental + incisive block (or full Spix)
📍
WhereMucobuccal fold past mesial root of lower M1 → into mental foramen
📏
DepthInto foramen (needle "fixes")
💧
Volume0.2 + 0.2 mL
TrapFibers cross the midline → contralateral labial plexal to catch residual.
8
Mental n.
Terminal branch of IAN (exits to soft tissue)
V3
Innervates: ½ lower lip, chin skin, buccal gingiva of lower I + C + P1.
🎯 The block
Mental + incisive block (same block, same site)
📍
WhereMucobuccal fold past mesial root of lower M1
🧭
DirectionDown + forward + inward
💧
Volume0.2 + 0.2 mL
TrapMental STOPS at the foramen — molar buccal is LBN territory.
D · V3 Support (lingual + long buccal)
9
LN
Lingual Nerve
V3
Innervates: lingual gingiva midline → M3, ant 2/3 tongue, floor of mouth.
🎯 The block
Spix T2 — or paralingual M3 — or Theodorescu at P2
📍
Spix T2Withdraw 1 cm from IAN deposit site — 2nd deposit
📍
Paralingual M3Paralingual fold next to lower M3
📍
TheodorescuFloor–mandible angle at P2
📏
Depth (isolated)0.5 cm — both variants
DangerLN is SUPERFICIAL distal to lower M3 — surgical injury zone.
10
LBN
Long Buccal Nerve (V3 anterior division)
V3
Innervates: buccal gingiva of lower molars + cheek mucosa.
🎯 The block
Long Buccal block (or vestibular infiltration distal to lower M3)
📍
WhereAnterior ramus × upper occlusal plane (base of coronoid)
📏
Depth2–4 mm
💧
Volume0.3–0.5 mL
Exam pointSpix ALONE does NOT cover molar buccal. Add LBN every mandibular molar.
