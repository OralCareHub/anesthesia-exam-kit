Plexal / Local infiltration
1. Plexal (supraperiosteal)
Default for upper teeth · supplement for lower incisors
Where to use
1.11.21.31.41.5 · 2.12.22.32.42.5 · 3.13.2 · 4.14.2
Site
Vestibular fornix / buccal sulcus above the tooth apex
Depth
Submucosal + supraperiosteal — few mm
Volume
0.6 mL
2. Plexal supplement at MB root of M1
The fix for the 1.6 / 2.6 trap (PSAN bypasses the MB root)
Where to use
1.6 · 2.6
Site
Vestibular fornix above the MB root of upper M1
Depth
Submucosal + supraperiosteal
Volume
0.6 mL
Always combined with the PSA tuberosity block when extracting upper M1.
3. Trans-frenular plexal
Catches midline crossover between the upper central incisors
Where to use
1.1 · 2.1
Site
Upper labial frenulum
Depth
Submucosal
Volume
0.3 mL
4. Local palatal infiltration
Small palatal top-up for 1–2 teeth
Where to use
1.3 · 2.3 (canine NPN ↔ GPN anastomosis zone)
Site
Palatal mucosa 3–5 mm from gingival groove of the tooth, toward midline
Depth
Submucosal
Volume
0.2 mL
Do NOT puncture the greater palatine artery — stay medial.
Maxillary blocks (V2 — peripheral trunk blocks)
5. PSA tuberosity block
Anesthetizes the Posterior Superior Alveolar nerve
Where to use
1.61.71.8 · 2.62.72.8 (remember: MB root of M1 needs separate plexal)
Site
Buccal sulcus near MB root of upper M2, behind the zygomatic process
Direction
Up 45° + inward + backward
Depth
10–15 mm to bone contact
Volume
~ 1 mL
Mandatory aspiration — pterygoid venous plexus → tuberosity hematoma. If positive: gauze + thenar compression in the superior fornix.
6. Infraorbital (IO) block
Single block for ASA (+ MSA when present) — covers incisors, canine, premolars
Where to use
1.41.5 · 2.42.5 (also covers I + C if extending block forward)
Site
Vestibular ~0.5 cm from buccal surface, parallel to long axis of upper P1; finger palpates IO foramen
Depth
1.5–2.5 cm to foramen
Volume
1.5–2 mL — small test, locate foramen, aspirate, slow inject
Extraoral variant (same nerve target)
Site
Skin: horizontal line through nasal wing, 0.5 cm lateral to nasolabial groove
Volume
0.2 mL test + 0.2 mL after needle fixes in foramen
NOT necessary to enter the canal.
7. Greater Palatine (GPN) block
Posterior 2/3 of hard palate — for all upper premolars + molars
Where to use
1.41.51.61.71.8 · 2.42.52.62.72.8
Site
Palatine mucoperiosteum, 1 cm medial to upper M3 alveolar ridge (mucosa looks paler / funnel-shaped at the foramen)
Approach
Syringe from the opposite commissure
Depth
Submucosal — do NOT enter the canal
Volume
0.2–0.3 mL ONLY
Over-injection → ischemic palatal necrosis. Stop at blanching.
8. Nasopalatine (NPN / Scarpa) block
Anterior 1/3 of hard palate — for upper canines + incisors
Where to use
1.11.21.3 · 2.12.22.3
Site
Palatal mucosa lateral to the incisive papilla — NEVER sting the papilla
Approach
From the side OPPOSITE the target tooth (V/Y canal)
Depth
5–6 mm into nasopalatine canal
Volume
0.2–0.3 mL
V/Y canal: entering from one side crosses to the contralateral nerve trunk — that's why opposite-side entry is mandatory.
Mandibular blocks (V3 — peripheral trunk blocks)
9. IAN block — Spix's spine technique
The workhorse — covers IAN + Lingual N in one approach
Where to use
3.13.23.33.43.53.63.73.8 · 4.14.24.34.44.54.64.74.8 (all 16 mandibular teeth)
Site
Lateral to pterygomandibular fold, medial to temporal crest, 1 cm above the occlusal plane
Syringe
Body lies over the OPPOSITE-side premolars
Direction
Posterior + lateral + horizontal
Depth
2–2.5 cm to bone contact
Volume
1–1.7 mL
Two deposits, two aspirations.
T1 — at bone contact: deposit for IAN.
T2 — withdraw 1 cm, aspirate again, deposit for Lingual n.
Facial palsy if needle passes posterior to ramus → parotid → CN VII anesthesia. Always confirm bone contact before depositing.
10. Mental + incisive block
Covers anterior mandible (canine + premolars → midline) without doing a full Spix
Where to use
3.33.4 · 4.34.4 (alternative to Spix for anterior teeth)
Site
Vestibular fornix at mesio-buccal root of lower M1; advance toward mental foramen
Depth
Locate foramen — needle becomes "fixed" once in canal
Volume
0.2 mL test (locate) + 0.2 mL deposit
Extraoral variant
Site
Cheek, 1.5–2 cm posterior to commissure of mouth on a horizontal line
Volume
0.2 mL test + 0.2 mL deposit
Aspirate before deposit. Mental foramen typically sits between roots of lower P1–P2.
11. Long Buccal nerve block
Mandatory companion to Spix for mandibular molar extractions
Where to use
3.63.73.8 · 4.64.74.8
Site
Anterior edge of vertical mandibular ramus, base of coronoid process
Approach
Syringe from the opposite commissure
Depth
2–4 mm
Volume
0.3–0.5 mL
Simple alternative — vestibular infiltration
Site
Vestibular mucosa distal and vestibular to lower M3
Depth
Submucosal + supraperiosteal
Volume
0.3–0.5 mL
Mental branch's gingival territory ENDS at the mental foramen — molar buccal gingiva is long buccal territory. Spix alone leaves it sensitive.
12. Lingual nerve block — isolated
Used when LN coverage is needed but Spix isn't done (or as supplement)
Where to use — M3 paralingual approach
3.8 · 4.8
Site
Paralingual fold next to lower M3, midway between gingiva and tongue base
Depth
0.5 cm
Volume
~ 0.5 mL
Dan Theodorescu technique — for anterior LN access
Where to use
3.33.5 · 4.34.5
Site
Angle between oral floor and lingual surface of mandible, next to canine or P2 (P2 = classic landmark)
Depth
0.5 cm
Volume
~ 0.5 mL
LN becomes superficial just distal to lower M3 — surgical danger zone, but easy to anesthetize.
